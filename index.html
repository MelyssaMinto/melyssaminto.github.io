I am Dr. Melyssa Minto, a genomic scientist working to understand how disease risk is encoded in our DNA and how external factors contribute to those risks. I am passionate about diversity within genomic studies and within the workforce. I bring a wealth of expertise and experience to this field including employing state-of-the-art computational tools and methodologies and domain expertise in neurobiology and psychiatric diseases. I also really love gardening and attempting to keep house plants alive.

Making Research a Career – Melyssa Minto

Date: May 2017

Melyssa Minto, ’17, a double major in mathematics and biology, is pursuing a Ph.D. in computational biology and bioinformatics at Duke University, driven by her passion for genetics, epigenetics, and research, while valuing the supportive Meredith College community that helped shape her academic and personal growth.

Projects

Cost-Efficient Compute Cluster

Created a cost-efficient compute cluster using AWS EC2 and Docker that uses SLURM workflow manager to parallelize statistical testing in a reproducible framework.

  • To analyze DNA methylation data across brain regions, identifying tissue-specific methylation patterns in alcohol use disorder [2]
  • Indentify Alcohol Use Disorder mQTLs across brain regions
Python AWS DNA Methylation

Gene-Network Replication Testing Framework

Developed a framework in R to test gene-network replication across studies, helping to identify target genes for drug repurposing in substance use disorders.
R Gene Network Analysis WGCNA

AI-Driven Summarization Tool

Led a team to create an AI tool for automating literature reviews, using PubMed queries, RAG workflows, and semantic clustering to enhance gene-disease association precision.
AI Python

eQTL Analysis for Biomarker Discovery

Conducted eQTL analysis using AWS and Plink2 to integrate genotype and methylation data, uncovering biomarkers for alcohol use disorder in scalable, reproducible pipelines.
eQTL AWS Plink2

Multi-omic Analysis of Transcription Factor Binding

Developed a multi-omic workflow in R to integrate gene expression and chromatin data, identifying transcription factors regulating neuronal plasticity via ChIP-seq data.
  • To identify neuronal cell-type specific addiction biomarkers [5]
  • To identify co-factors of Zics that mediate cerebellar development [3]
Multi-omics TF Binding R ChIP-seq

MonoInc R Package

Developed an R package that incliudes various imputation methods are utilized in this package, where one can flag and impute non-monotonic data that is outside of a prespecified range.
